The RCFTO Framework — Role · Context · Format · Tone · Output
R
Role
The precise perspective your writing comes from — not "expert" but the specific angle only you hold
C
Context
The reader's exact situation — their beliefs, frustrations, and what they need to feel understood
F
Format
The architecture of your argument — the deliberate sequence that builds to your conclusion
T
Tone
Your specific voice — sentence rhythm, language preference, register — not "professional" but unmistakably you
O
Output
What the reader should know, feel, believe, and do by the last word — the standard everything is measured against
